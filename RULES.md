# Into the Wild TCG

## Overview

Into the Wild TCG is a card game about building and maintaining an ecosystem. Players develop habitats, manage bio-energy, and use organisms and environmental events to influence the state of play. The game is currently being developed with a broad audience in mind, including youth and adults.

This ruleset is tentative and may change as the game is tested.

## Design Goals

The current design direction is to support:

- a broad audience
- simple core rules
- clear card roles
- ecosystem-themed strategy
- replayable card interactions
- a ruleset that is easy to adjust during development

The game is intended to be approachable for new players while still leaving room for deeper play through card combinations and timing.

## Card Types

The current design uses four main card types:

- **Bio-Energy**
- **Habitat**
- **Organism**
- **Environmental Event**

These categories may be refined as development continues.

## Bio-Energy

Bio-Energy cards represent the resources used to support other cards.

The current resource types are:

- Water
- Flora
- Fauna

Bio-Energy cards may:
- provide resources
- refresh over time
- support the play of other cards

## Habitat

Habitat cards represent the spaces and conditions that support life in the ecosystem.

Habitat cards may:
- help support organisms
- improve ecosystem stability
- provide structure for card interactions

## Organism

Organism cards represent living creatures in the ecosystem.

Organism cards may:
- interact with opposing cards through challenges
- depend on suitable resources or habitats
- contribute to endgame scoring or ecosystem balance

## Environmental Event

Environmental Event cards represent temporary shifts in the ecosystem.

Event cards may:
- modify play conditions
- influence resources or stability
- affect both players
- create changing board states

## Deck Construction

A deck currently consists of **52 cards**.

The exact number of cards in each category is still open and may change as testing continues. The goal is to find a structure that supports both clarity and variety.

## Game Areas

Each player may use the following areas:

- **Deck**
- **Canopy**
- **Habitat**
- **Bio-Energy Pool**
- **Compost Pile**
- **Exile**

These areas may be adjusted if playtesting suggests a cleaner layout.

## Core Actions

The current design uses the following actions:

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Play** | Move a card from the Canopy into the appropriate play area. |
| **Challenge** | Attempt to interact with an opponent's Bio-Energy or Habitat. |
| **Protect** | Prevent, redirect, or modify the outcome of a Challenge. |
| **Resolve** | Apply the result of a Challenge or ability. |
| **Refresh** | Ready exhausted cards. |
| **Draw** | Draw the top card of your Deck. |
| **Reveal** | Show a card without changing its location. |
| **Search** | Look through a Deck for a matching card, then shuffle. |
| **Shuffle** | Randomize the order of a Deck. |
| **Restore** | Return a card from Compost to its owner's Deck, then shuffle. |
| **Target** | Select a card or player affected by an action. |

## Challenge Outcomes

A Challenge may result in one or more of the following outcomes:

| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Consume** | Compost an opponent's Bio-Energy card. |
| **Compost** | Move a card to its owner's Compost pile. |
| **Return** | Return a card to its owner's Canopy. |
| **Exhaust** | Turn a card sideways until it Refreshes. |
| **Exile** | Remove a card from the game. |
| **Cancel** | Prevent a Challenge or ability from resolving. |

## Rank-Based Card Mapping

For prototyping, the game may use a familiar rank-based structure.

- **Aces** may represent apex predators or top-tier ecosystem threats.
- **Kings** may represent dominant or territorial species.
- **Queens** may represent adaptable or highly influential species.
- **Jacks** may represent support, defense, or utility roles.
- **Number cards** may represent the remaining organisms, habitats, and resources.
- **Jokers** may represent Environmental Events or wildcard effects.

This mapping is intended as a flexible design guide and may be revised.

## Turn Structure

A turn may follow this general sequence:

1. **Restore** exhausted cards.
2. **Draw** a card.
3. **Play** cards.
4. **Challenge** opposing cards or conditions.
5. **Resolve** abilities and outcomes.
6. **End** the turn.

The exact order and timing of steps may be adjusted if a cleaner flow is found during testing.

## Endgame Direction

The current idea is to score the game using the cards that remain in play at the end of the game.

A simple rank-based scoring model may be used as a starting point, with optional adjustments for ecosystem stability, support, or synergy.

Example scoring values:
- Ace = 5
- King = 4
- Queen = 3
- Jack = 2
- Number card = 1
- Joker = variable or special case

Possible modifiers may include:
- bonuses for supported cards
- penalties for unsupported cards
- bonuses for completed ecosystem relationships
- penalties for unstable or disconnected states

The final scoring system is still tentative and may be simplified or expanded depending on test results.

## Development Notes

This ruleset is intentionally provisional. The current goal is to find a version of the game that is easy to learn, enjoyable to play, and clear enough to test repeatedly.

As development continues, individual card templates, challenge timing, scoring rules, and event behavior may all change.
